90-Day Activation Roadmap

Weeks 1-4 · Diagnose

Embed, Listen, Map

  • Meet with Lead of Global Remote Operations, then every working group member (Security, IT, Legal, business function leaders)
  • Inventory current CoE assets: tool library, use policy, intake process, governance framework, enablement materials
  • Run quick-diagnostic surveys or interviews with 20-30 goats across functions to validate the readiness hypothesis
  • Identify the 5-8 highest-leverage Champions Network candidates
Weeks 4-10 · Deliver

Ship One Program End-to-End

  • Launch Wave 1 activation (likely Sales & SE or CS) with function-specific playbook, training, and AI tool access
  • Stand up measurement framework before launch, not after (adoption KPIs, impact metrics, behavior change indicators)
  • Onboard the first cohort of Champions with a clear cadence and sustainment plan
  • Evolve one or two CoE assets (tool library or use policy) based on diagnosis
Weeks 10-90-Day Mark · Scale

Translate Insights into CoE Strategy

  • Present diagnostic findings and Wave 1 outcomes to VP and C-suite stakeholders with prioritized roadmap
  • Expand Champions Network to Wave 2 functions with activation playbooks ready
  • Establish CoE operating cadence (working group rhythm, stakeholder reporting, roadmap reviews)
  • Begin shaping the multi-quarter CoE investment thesis for leadership

1AI Tool Library

  • Living, searchable catalog of approved AI tools by function
  • Clear approval tier (Open / Reviewed / Restricted) per tool
  • Use case examples and playbooks attached to each entry
  • Refreshed monthly based on Champions Network feedback

2Use Policy

  • Plain-language guidance, not legal jargon
  • Clear do's and don'ts mapped to common workflows
  • Data classification requirements (what can go where)
  • Escalation path for edge cases and new use cases

3Intake Process

  • Lightweight request form, not a gauntlet
  • SLA commitment for initial triage (48 hours max)
  • Working group routing for complex or sensitive asks
  • Public backlog so goats see what's in motion

4Champions Network

  • One visible champion per function, peer-selected
  • Monthly cadence: training, use case sharing, office hours
  • Quarterly offsite or virtual event with executive sponsor
  • Recognition and career development tied to champion role

Measurement Framework

Programs that aren't measured don't earn the right to grow. Three tiers of metrics, each answering a different question leadership will ask.

Tier 1 · Adoption

Is AI being used?

  • Monthly active users of approved AI tools, by function
  • Champions Network engagement (training attendance, use case submissions)
  • Intake volume and approval rate trend
  • Tool library utilization by category
Tier 2 · Behavior Change

Is AI changing how work gets done?

  • Workflow integration depth (one-off vs. embedded in daily process)
  • Self-reported time savings per workflow, validated against baseline
  • Quality of output before vs. after AI integration (function-specific rubrics)
  • Sustainment rate (are users still using the tool 90 days post-activation?)
Tier 3 · Business Impact

Is AI moving the numbers?

  • Cycle time reduction on key workflows (e.g., sales cycle, support resolution)
  • Capacity freed up for higher-leverage work, measured in FTE-equivalent hours
  • Function-level OKR contribution attributed to AI activation
  • Cost savings vs. alternative approaches (hiring, vendors, manual effort)
