The Feedback Loop

📖 8 min read ✏️ 1 exercise Module 1, Lesson 5 of 6

Effective feedback isn't a one-time event — it's a continuous cycle of observation, reflection, delivery, and follow-up. Organizations that build strong feedback cultures don't just tell people to "give more feedback." They create systems, habits, and safe spaces that make feedback a natural part of how work gets done.

The Four Stages

Every meaningful feedback interaction follows a predictable pattern, whether you realize it or not:

  • Observe: Pay attention to specific behaviors, outcomes, and patterns — not personality traits or assumptions about intent.
  • Reflect: Before speaking, ask yourself what outcome you want. Is this feedback for them, or for you? What's the most helpful framing?
  • Deliver: Share the feedback using a clear framework (we'll cover SBI in Module 2). Be direct, specific, and kind.
  • Follow Up: Check back in. Did the feedback land? Did anything change? This step is where most people drop the ball.
💡 Key Insight: Research consistently shows that the follow-up step is what separates feedback that sticks from feedback that's forgotten. A 2-minute check-in a week later is worth more than a perfectly crafted initial delivery.

Common Pitfalls

Even well-intentioned feedback can go sideways. Here are the patterns to watch for:

  • The Sandwich: Positive-negative-positive might feel safe, but most people see through it. It dilutes both the praise and the constructive point.
  • The Drive-By: Dropping feedback in a Slack message or at the end of an unrelated meeting. Feedback deserves its own space.
  • The Historian: Saving up months of observations and delivering them all at once during a review cycle. Feedback should be timely.

Making It Stick

The strongest feedback cultures we've seen share three traits: leaders who model receiving feedback publicly, structured moments (like retrospectives or 1:1s) where feedback is expected, and low-stakes practice environments where people can build the muscle without high consequences.
